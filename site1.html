1. Mount Fuji

Mount Fuji is usually the first thought when it comes to sightseeing in Japan. The mountain is a breathtaking 3,776 meters tall! A must for anyone visiting near Tokyo!

2. Tokyo Imperial Palace

Another must see near Tokyo if you are interested in the history of Japan is the Imperial Palace. This is where the Emperer of Japan would reside and hold ceremonies for various events! Besides the historical significance it is simply beautiful to look at!

3. Fushimi Inari-taisha Shrine

Fushimi Inari-taisha is a shrine located in Kyoto, Japan. It rests near the base of the mountain Inari and is a very interesting sight to see. For more insight into the history of this shrine visit this site!

4. Hiroshima Peace Memorial Park

Hiroshima Peace Memorial Park is a memorial in Hiroshima that is dedicated to the first city to ever suffer a nuclear attack. As most of us know there were two nuclear bombs dropped at the end of Wrodl War 2 and Hiroshima was the first to be impacted. The park is in an open field that was created by the explosion. A very dark but interesting place to visit and learn the real consequences of this horrific event.

5. Itsukushima Shrine

Itsukushima Shrine is another shrine similar to the Fushimi Inari-taisha Shrine. This one is located near Hiroshima and would be a great place to visit if you are already visiting the Hiroshima Peace Memorial. This shirne is located in shallow waters which adds to its beauty appeal. To learn more of the historical significane of this shirine visit this site!

6. The Landscape of Japan

This last section is less specific than the last 5. It serves as a reminder that the landscape of Japan is an attraction in and of itself! The endless grasslands and colorful flora is truly a sight to behold. For a closer look at the images above, click on them!
